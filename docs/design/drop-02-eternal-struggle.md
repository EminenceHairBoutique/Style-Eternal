# DROP 02 — ETERNAL STRUGGLE

## Design System

---

## Concept Line
> "Power breaks. Unity survives. The struggle is the style."

Eternal Struggle is about the tension between systems of power and the people who endure them. It is not a protest collection — it is an aesthetic statement about permanence under pressure. The graphics speak to rebellion, but through controlled symbolism, not literal narrative.

---

## Emotional Tone
- Power
- Pressure
- Permanence
- Rebellion
- Unity
- Emotional heaviness
- Controlled aggression
- Quiet defiance

## Preferred Motifs

### Hero Symbols
1. **Cracked Crown** — A crown fractured down the center. Power that doesn't hold.
2. **Broken Scales** — Scales of justice tilted, cracked, or dissolving. System failure.
3. **Smoke Halo** — A halo made of smoke or vapor above a silhouette. Divinity under pressure.
4. **Abstract Surveillance Eye** — Fragmented eye shapes suggesting being watched. Not literal camera/CCTV.
5. **Silhouettes in Unity** — Two or more figures standing together, abstracted. Solidarity without identity.

### Supporting Symbols
- Barbed wire accents (thin, architectural — not aggressive)
- Chain links (broken, not intact)
- Abstract city grid lines
- Cracked concrete textures
- Vertical scan lines / static overlay

## Typography Hierarchy

### Primary Title
- **Font:** Oswald 700 or custom condensed gothic
- **Size:** Dominant — fills chest or back panel
- **Style:** All caps, tracked 0.04–0.08em
- **Examples:** "ETERNAL STRUGGLE", "NO KINGS", "BORN AGAINST POWER"

### Secondary Title
- **Font:** Space Grotesk 500
- **Size:** Supporting — beneath or above primary title
- **Style:** All caps, wide tracking 0.15–0.25em, smaller scale
- **Examples:** "STYLE ETERNAL", "SS26", "DROP 02"

### Body/Motto Text
- **Font:** Inter 400 italic or Space Grotesk 400
- **Size:** Small — accent line on back or sleeve
- **Style:** Sentence case or all caps at very small size
- **Examples:** "power breaks. unity survives.", "est. 2021 — newark, nj"

## Hero Graphic System

### Composition Rules
1. Title is always the dominant element — never subordinate to illustration
2. Symbols are supporting, not competing with text
3. Central vertical axis alignment for all hero graphics
4. Maximum 3 elements per composition (title + symbol + accent)
5. Negative space is critical — never fill every inch
6. Grain/texture overlay at 10–20% opacity for depth

### Scale Guidelines
- Back graphics: 12–14" wide, 14–16" tall maximum
- Chest graphics: 3–4" wide maximum
- Sleeve graphics: 2–3" wide, runs length of forearm
- Thigh graphics: 1.5–2" wide maximum

| Position | Purpose | Scale |
|----------|---------|-------|
| **Front Chest (Left)** | Brand mark / monogram | Small (2–3") |
| **Front Chest (Center)** | Title-led statement graphic | Medium (8–10") |
| **Back** | Hero graphic — primary design canvas | Large (12–16") |
| **Left Sleeve** | Drop identifier or pattern | Thin vertical |
| **Right Sleeve** | Brand text or accent | Thin vertical |
| **Hood Interior** | SE embroidery (premium detail) | Tiny (1") |
| **Hem/Label** | Woven label, garment details | Standard |

## What to Avoid

- ❌ Literal police/protest imagery (too divisive, too literal)
- ❌ Specific political figures or party references
- ❌ Fist iconography (already used in Freedom Hoodie — do not repeat)
- ❌ Text-heavy compositions with paragraphs on garments
- ❌ Red/pink tones (reserved for Drop 03)
- ❌ Photorealistic illustration
- ❌ Graffiti/tag-style lettering
- ❌ Overly aggressive/violent imagery
- ❌ Direct Newark references in graphics (keep universal)
- ❌ More than 3 print colors per garment
